---
title: Proof of the existence of Ein Sof 
date: 2026-06-03
---

1. Assume that G-D is infinite, and can transcend all things. We also assume, trivially, that everything in the Torah does not contradict itself (we assume this is a logical impossibility, as the Torah is "perfect"). 

2. Note that we live in a universe made out of matter. We have two subcases corresponding to the different nature of the universe:

a. If the universe is infinite, then G-D supercedes that "infinity" because we assume He can transcend all things, so it is always true that G-D "encircles" this space. 

b. The universe is finite, in which case G-D encasing this is trivial. 

3. So, it is implied that anything we see and can imagine is encircled bhy the nature of G-D. 

4. So either our existence is within:

a. Some space within G-D, i.e. we are integrated components within his "space", but this contradicts our assumption that G-D can transcend all things, as if we are within him He is not transcended but rather on the same plane of material existence, so this cannot be true. 

b. Or, there exists some space created by G-D, that is separate from his nature but also derived from his powers, in which we reside. Essentially, the complement of the set of G-D that G-D also created. 

5. So, it is implied that we are in the space created by G-D. 

6. Take the first few passages of Genesis. It is stated that there existed nothing but "formlessness and emptiness, darkness". Because G-D is omnipotent and the creator of all things, and because this space has to have been derived from G-D, we can assume that G-D created this "darkness" from within himself. Additionally, G-D also emanated the light from Himself because He has to have created everything. 

7. The world is, according to the passage, derived from this synthesis between the darkness and light. 

8. So we have proved that, the world in which we exist is separate from G-D but also derived from His powers, that this world was created through the meticulous interaction between Light and Darkness ("separated the light and dark"). 

9. We have also proved that the universe we live in is "finite" relative to G-D's infinity, because he encircles everything. So because this universe is finite, G-D must have had to, in the process of creation, utilize His "infinity" to transform to the "finite", and manipulate this "finite" in such a way that is representative of the creative process. 

10. So, there exists some point in time, or some point in the number line when we can "transition" from "infinite" to "finite", which further necessitates the subcomponents of this light into different pieces in order to make it fininte. 

11. So, finally, we have proved the necessity of Ein Sof, as it relates to analyzing and understanding the transition between the infinite and finite, given our priors.
